================================================================================
  ALBERTON TYRE CLINIC — WORKSHOP & PREMISES LAYOUT (visual reference)
  Based on site photos (March 2026). Use for image prompts, briefs, and brand docs.
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The facility mixes three main zones: (1) a large open service / tyre bay with built-in
lifts, (2) secondary bays with heavy tyre machinery against painted walls, and (3) a
customer-facing reception / rim showroom with face-brick and a feature rim wall. The
overall look is industrial-professional: working garage, not a glossy studio—dust on
mats, tools, real wear, fluorescent light plus daylight from roll-up doors.


FLOORING (throughout workshop areas)
-------------------------------------
• Black interlocking industrial rubber tiles covering the whole work floor.
• Raised “coin” or circular stud texture (anti-slip, heavy-duty).
• Visible seams between tiles; light dust, debris, and traffic wear—authentic busy shop.
• Integrated into the floor: large rectangular BRIGHT ORANGE metal lift platforms /
  ramps (flush or low-profile)—strong brand-colour anchor against the black floor.
• In some bays: small metal floor hatches or drainage-style plates; black positioning
  blocks / sensors near alignment areas.


WALLS — PAINTED WORKSHOP BAYS
------------------------------
• Three-band scheme on many internal walls:
  – Lower third (~waist height): dark charcoal / blue-grey.
  – Middle: thin horizontal SAFETY ORANGE stripe (brand line).
  – Upper: light cool grey or off-white.
• Matte paint; visible conduits, sockets, switch plates, hose hooks—typical workshop.
• Optional accent: section of wall painted solid black (rim display) with band of blue
  below on one run.
• Posters / signage: Hankook (white panel, orange/black logo), Maxxis (banner with 4x4
  and tread imagery), tyre-tread close-up poster, Pirelli sign (yellow/red) in customer
  areas; Goodyear visible from some angles toward exterior.


WALLS — BRICK BAYS & RECEPTION
------------------------------
• Exposed red / brown FACE BRICK in reception and at least one service bay that opens
  toward an outdoor courtyard.
• Waist-high brick partition with smooth grey concrete capping—acts as counter between
  workshop and office / reception.
• Behind reception: feature wall often split matte BLACK (upper) and deep BLUE (lower),
  used to mount many alloy rims (silver and black finishes) in rows.


CEILING & LIGHTING
------------------
• Flat light grey or white ceiling panels (commercial / drop-ceiling style in places).
• Long rectangular fluorescent tube fixtures in parallel rows—bright, even, “clinical”
  workshop light.
• Large open roll-up / garage doorways: strong natural daylight from one side, high
  contrast between bright exterior and slightly darker interior.


LAYOUT — MAIN WORKSHOP
----------------------
• Open plan: machines and storage along walls; centre kept clear for vehicles over lifts.
• Back and side walls: line of red-and-black tyre changers / balancers (e.g. Corghi-style
  red bases, M.B. Engineering TC 525 PG, Hunter DSP9600 wheel balancer with red front,
  digital head, black hood, spindle, tray of stick-on weights on floor).
• Tyre storage: stacks of tyres on the floor; metal rack on wall with tyres; loose
  groupings near machines.
• Rim display: many alloys mounted on black wall section (left side in wide shots).
• Fire extinguisher (red) on wall; brooms, buckets, small tools, drink cans—lived-in.
• Staff: black work uniforms (dark tees and trousers) in active work shots.


LAYOUT — BAY OPENING TO OUTSIDE
--------------------------------
• Wide opening to paved courtyard; exterior building visible with light walls and
  horizontal orange stripe (matches interior branding).
• Vehicles may be parked outside (e.g. pickup, SUV)—context for “real dealership /
  fitment centre” environment.


LAYOUT — RECEPTION / SHOWROOM
------------------------------
• L-shaped reception counter: face-brick base, thick polished grey CONCRETE countertop.
• High wooden bar stools with woven seats along part of the counter.
• Large vertical Maxxis stand; tyres set into recessed niches in brick counter front;
  BFGoodrich rack visible through openings; restroom signage on brick.
• Computers and POS materials on counter; rim wall as main visual backdrop.


COLOUR & MOOD (for prompts)
---------------------------
• Dominant: black rubber floor, charcoal lower walls, light grey upper walls.
• Accent: orange (stripe + floor lifts) and safety red (machinery).
• Materials: matte metal, rubber, brick, concrete—not CGI gloss.
• Atmosphere: functional, high-traffic, South African tyre fitment centre—real light,
  real texture, documentary feel.
